Why it matters

The problem we're solving

Bengaluru maintains hundreds of public toilets, but upkeep is reactive: a facility is cleaned on a fixed roster or after citizens complain. Between those moments, hygiene silently degrades — and usable public sanitation directly affects public health, dignity and how the city is experienced by women, children and gig workers who depend on it daily.

Sense continuously

Each facility carries an ESP32 node reading ammonia (odour), stall occupancy, footfall, water-tank and soap levels — every few seconds, around the clock.

Alert automatically

A rule engine turns raw readings into actionable alerts — high NH₃, low supplies, offline nodes — routed to supervisors with acknowledge & dispatch workflows.

Decide with data

Footfall curves, cleanliness trends and refill forecasts let the civic body schedule crews and consumables where the city actually needs them.

From sensor to action

How SwachhSense works

Sense

MQ-137 gas, ultrasonic level and IR footfall sensors sample each facility in real time.

Transmit

The ESP32 node packages readings as JSON telemetry packets and streams them to the platform.

Evaluate

The rule engine scores cleanliness and raises alerts the moment thresholds are crossed.

Act

Supervisors acknowledge alerts, dispatch crews and log maintenance — all from the dashboard.

This deployment monitors 6 pilot facilities across Bengaluru — Majestic, Cubbon Park, KR Market, Indiranagar Metro, Jayanagar 4th Block and MG Road. In this academic prototype the sensor hardware is simulated in software, but the data model matches the JSON packet a physical ESP32 node would send. Browse the facility network →
